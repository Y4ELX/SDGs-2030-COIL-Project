Life Below Water

Goal 14 of the UN Sustainable Development Goals aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources. Oceans cover three quarters of the Earth's surface, contain 97% of the Earth's water, and represent 99% of the living space on the planet by volume.

More than three billion people depend on marine and coastal biodiversity for their livelihoods. However, 30% of the world's fish stocks are overexploited, reaching below the level at which they can produce sustainable yields.

Human activities like overfishing, plastic pollution and ocean acidification are endangering this valuable resource. It's essential we take urgent action to preserve marine ecosystems through sustainable fishing policies, pollutant reduction and marine protected areas.

Actions You Can Take

Reduce Plastic Use

Choose reusable bags, bottles, and containers. Avoid single-use plastics like straws and cutlery. Participate in beach cleanups to prevent plastic from entering the ocean.

Sustainable Seafood

Choose seafood from sustainable sources. Look for MSC (Marine Stewardship Council) or ASC (Aquaculture Stewardship Council) certified products. Avoid overfished species.

Reduce Chemical Pollution

Use eco-friendly cleaning products and minimize fertilizer use. Properly dispose of chemicals and medications to prevent them from entering waterways.

Support Marine Conservation

Donate to or volunteer with organizations protecting oceans. Advocate for marine protected areas and sustainable fishing policies in your community.

Reduce Carbon Footprint

Ocean acidification from CO2 emissions harms marine life. Use energy-efficient appliances, reduce car trips, and support renewable energy initiatives.

Educate Others

Share knowledge about marine conservation. Teach children about the importance of healthy oceans and how to protect them.

No Poverty

Goal 1 of the SDGs focuses on eradicating poverty in all its forms everywhere. Although the number of people living in extreme poverty decreased by more than half between 1990 and 2015, too many are still struggling to meet the most basic human needs.

In 2015, about 736 million people lived on less than $1.90 a day. Poverty isn't just measured by income but also by limited access to health services, education, clean water and sanitation, and a life free from violence and discrimination.

Economic growth must be inclusive, with sustainable jobs that promote equality. Social protection systems need to be implemented to help mitigate the effects of disasters and support those who cannot support themselves. Investing in education, health and nutrition is key to breaking the intergenerational cycle of poverty.

Actions You Can Take

Support Fair Trade

Purchase fair trade products to ensure producers in developing countries receive fair wages. Look for Fairtrade International or similar certifications.

Volunteer Locally

Donate time to food banks, shelters, or job training programs. Mentor youth from disadvantaged backgrounds to help break the cycle of poverty.

Responsible Donations

Research and support effective charities fighting poverty. Consider microfinance donations that help people start small businesses.

Advocate for Policy Change

Support policies that increase access to education, healthcare, and living wages. Contact representatives about anti-poverty legislation.

Ethical Employment

If you're an employer, pay living wages and provide benefits. Support employee education and advancement opportunities.

Financial Literacy

Teach financial management skills in your community. Many poverty cycles continue due to lack of knowledge about saving and investing.
